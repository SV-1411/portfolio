    GML (Gigzs Memory Layer) is a unique concept designed to make AI work more like a human.
    This post shows a visual overview of how GML works internally. The actual system is much more complex, but at its core, GML acts as a live memory graph connected to AI agents. It continuously uses current context, past conversations, and shared resources to respond more accurately.
    In simple terms, AI cannot perform well without context. When context is missing, AI tends to hallucinate and fails to reach its full potential. GML solves this by storing and maintaining long-term context, allowing AI to function properly and consistently.
    At Gigzs, we are taking this idea to the next level. GML is designed to connect with any AI system and preserve context over long periods. We are currently testing it rigorously, with plans to launch an enterprise version in 2026.
    Some people asked, “Gigzs is about freelancing, so how is GML related?”
    GML was built specifically for the Gigzs marketplace. It strengthens our Quick SaaS Commerce vertical by solving one of the biggest freelancing problems: context loss. GML stores a business’s DNA for the long term, reducing repeated briefings and saving time for both clients and freelancers.
    When we say we are building an AI-powered freelancing marketplace, this is exactly what we mean.
